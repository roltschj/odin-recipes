Perfect Tofu

OBLIGATORY CONTENT PRECEDING USEFUL INFORMATION

Tofu has somewhat of a tarnished reputation among those who, understandably, have not had reason to seek out the well-prepared variety. Regarding first experiences, mine, too, was that of a flavorless, wriggly mass of protein that felt out of place and unnatural. It wasn't until many years later, when I decided to prepare it myself and sought out the proper method, that I learned something that whoever ran that forgettable restaurant never did: PRESS YOUR BEAN CURD. Yum, man!

INGREDIENTS

  • One (1) 14 oz. block firm or extra firm tofu
  • 1 1/2 tbsp oil- Preferably one with a high smoke point. Avocado, canola, peanut, etc.
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 oz. vinegar-based hot sauce (Frank's is a reliable choice)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • A stack of heavy books (if not using a tofu press)

THE METHOD

  1. Before we do anything in the pan, we need to get as much water as we can out of our tofu. Think of it like a sponge- the more water we squeeze out of it, the more readily it will absorb the flavor of our liquid ingredients, and the more satisfying crisp we can achieve. You can, of course, purchase "pre-squeezed" tofu for a bit more money. That's up to you. If, like me, you don't mind a little extra work, keep reading. Otherwise skip to step two.

    Proper Pressing

    There are two main ways to press tofu: with a tofu press, or with absorbent material- either a very clean towel, or (more commonly) a lot of paper towel. If you have a tofu press, simply follow the instructions. Otherwise, wrap your towel (or 12-20 sheets of the paper stuff) around your block, place the bundle on top of a clean surface (I prefer a bamboo cutting board), another clean, flat, water resistant surface atop the bundle (I have in the past used folded cereal boxes), and a stack of heavy books on top of all that. Phew! Make sure it's structurally sound- a falling stack of books can be dangerous- and let it press. Check on it every ten minutes or so; if your absorbent material is waterlogged, it's a good idea to change it out for more. After about half an hour, you may consider your tofu pressed.

  2. Start heating oil on medium-high and cut your tofu into roughly 1/2" cubes.
  3. Test your oil temp by tossing a single tofu cube into the pan. When you hear the sizzle, it's go time. Toss all the tofu into the pan, and continuously stir and toss the cubes. The idea here is to heat them evenly, or evenly enough, to keep them from sticking to the pan. Once the outer edges crisp up a bit, we can relax.
  4. Lower to medium heat and toss in remaining ingredients. Stir to distribute sauce and spices evenly.
  5. Let cook for ten minutes or cubes begin to brown, stirring occasionally.
  6. Enjoy your perfect tofu with some rice or perhaps toast!
